Christopher Buecheler:</p>

I think the thing that frustrates me the most about it is: they’ll probably get a thousand applicants. A bunch of 25 year-old kids with a ton of talent and stars in their eyes are going to try to get this crap job for crap pay so they can work somewhere “cool” and feel like a part of something big.</p>
</blockquote>

This is a big problem in the industry, and not isolated to Penny Arcade. I turn down unsolicited offers from startups all the time simply because they promise that they'll make up for the lack of decent pay by providing all sorts of "intangibles" and a great "work-life balance" and so on.</p>

But it's a big problem at bigger companies, too. Companies like Apple.</p>
