Fairfield University psychologist Linda Henkel:</p>

You're just kind of mentally discounting it--thinking, 'Well, the camera's got it.'</p>
</blockquote>

I've noticed this in my own work, and that's part of the reason I've neglected my photography for the last few years. I broke my Nikon a while back and, while waiting for its repair, noticed that I was enjoying travel a lot better when I wasn't constantly looking at life like a photographer. I took trip to Paris a while back, and all I really remember from it now are what I see in the pictures I took; I don't want that to happen to my time with my son while he grows up.</p>
