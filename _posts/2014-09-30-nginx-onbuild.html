I needed a lightweight nginx setup that I just throw config files into, thus was born docker-nginx-onbuild</a>. The base container will add any files in the context to /etc/nginx</code> and your CMD</code> need only be arguments to nginx</code>.</p>

To build our nginx container here at Djed</a>, my docker context has a few files:</p>

  • conf.d/site.conf</li>
  • ssl/certificate.crt</li>
  • ssl/certificate.key</li>
    </ul>

    My Dockerfile</code> is very simple and looks like this:</p>

    FROM jcderr/nginx-onbuild:latest
    
MAINTAINER Jeremy Derr my@email
    
CMD [ "-g", "daemon off;" ]
    
</code></pre>
    

    site.conf</code> defines my upstreams (which are sockets exported by other containers) and my hosts, and that's about it.</p>
